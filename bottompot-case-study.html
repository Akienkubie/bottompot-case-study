01 — The Problem

Port Harcourt's Food Scene Has Energy. It Lacks Infrastructure.

Port Harcourt is one of Nigeria's most commercially active cities — oil money, night life, a food culture rooted in Niger Delta tradition. But beneath the surface, most food businesses operate on instinct. No structured costing. No digital ordering. No data. No loyalty systems.

Restaurant owners work long hours with tight margins and no tools built for their context. Customers love the food but have no way to track orders, earn rewards, or know what's coming next.

And when a restaurant wants to grow — wants to become an experience, not just a meal — there is no operational backbone to support that ambition. Until now.

02 — The Founder
Chef · Entrepreneur · System Architect

He Didn't Apply for the Job.
He Cooked His Way In.

Akien Kubie — known in the creative world as TAMUNIUM — is a professional chef and founder with deep roots in Nigerian cuisine, particularly the flavours and traditions of the Niger Delta. He didn't take a conventional path into the Port Harcourt food industry.

At Rubicon Restaurant Bar and Lounge in Trans Amadi, he passed a cooking test using a technique he had discovered and named himself: the Double Coconut Method — rendering coconut oil from fresh coconut water to toast rice before cooking, creating a depth of flavour that standard cooking methods cannot replicate.

That test earned him employment. But employment was never the destination — it was the launchpad.

The Double Coconut Method

A self-developed culinary technique in which coconut oil is rendered directly from fresh coconut water to toast rice before cooking. The result is a complex, layered flavour profile that cannot be achieved through conventional methods — and a signature that belongs exclusively to the Bottom Pot kitchen.

Akien is not just a chef working at Rubicon. He is the CEO of Bottom Pot — an African SME Business Operating System built on AppSheet and Google Sheets — using his chef income to fund the platform's development while building its first public activation inside the very restaurant that hired him.

03 — The Activation

Bottom Pot Live Kitchen
at Rubicon — Every Saturday.

The Concept

Live. Outdoor. Communal.

The Bottom Pot Live Kitchen is a weekly outdoor cooking experience hosted at Rubicon's premises every Saturday. Guests don't just eat — they watch, engage, and participate in a curated culinary event built around Nigerian flavour, live fire, and the story behind each dish.

The Model

Revenue-Sharing, Not Just a Pop-Up.

Bottom Pot and Rubicon operate under a structured arrangement — the platform drives audience, handles digital ordering and tracking, and receives a platform fee per transaction. Rubicon provides the venue, kitchen infrastructure, and staff. Both parties grow together.

The Technology

Every Order Is a Data Point.

Every transaction at the Live Kitchen runs through the Bottom Pot platform — built on a 17-table Google Sheets master database with AppSheet front-end. Customer orders, cook performance, rider dispatch, and revenue tracking are all captured and usable from day one.

Launch Target

Saturday, April 18, 2026.

The first Bottom Pot Live Kitchen activation is targeted for Saturday, April 18, 2026 — a defined, costed, publicly committed milestone. The date is not a hope. It is a plan with a cost model behind it.

What Makes This Different

Most restaurant pop-ups are events. The Bottom Pot Live Kitchen is an infrastructure test. It is the first real-world deployment of the Bottom Pot platform — a weekly stress test of the ordering system, the logistics model, the cost structure, and the brand, in front of a live Port Harcourt audience.

Every Saturday becomes a dataset. Every dataset improves the platform. Every improvement makes the system more attractive to the next hotel, restaurant, or food entrepreneur who joins Bottom Pot's ecosystem.

06 — What's Next

After Rubicon,
Every Restaurant
Is a Candidate.

The Live Kitchen activation at Rubicon is not a one-off campaign. It is Bottom Pot's proof-of-concept deployment — designed to demonstrate that the platform can power a real hospitality business in Port Harcourt's competitive market.

Following the April 11 launch, the roadmap includes: platform expansion to additional restaurants and hotels in Port Harcourt, formal CAC incorporation of Bottom Pot Limited, growth of the Farmer Marketplace and Akawo savings features, and exploration of IoT smart-room integration for hotel clients.

The goal is full platform independence within 12 months — funded by Rubicon income, proven by Live Kitchen data, and scaled through the relationships this activation builds.
